Abstract

The Monumentenwacht Vlaanderen Tasks ontology (MW-T) is a taxonomy containing OWL classes to classify **construction reparation tasks**, extending [cto:ReparationTask](https://w3id.org/cto#ReparationTask). The taxonomy is manually created based on existing PDF reports of Monumentenwacht regarding damage inspections. In its current state, it contains preventive and curative repair tasks regarding timber damage [1] and damage caused by insects [2]. The main taxonomy is structured using RDFS subsumption axioms.

The namespace for MW-T terms is https://w3id.org/con-tax/tasks/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen#

The suggested prefix for the MW-T taxonomy is mw-t

The Turtle version of the MW-T taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/tasks/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen.ttl
The RDF/XML version of the MW-T taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/tasks/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen.rdf
The JSON-LD version of the MW-T taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/tasks/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen.jsonld
The N-Triples version of the MW-T taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/tasks/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen/monumentenwacht-vlaanderen.nt

The MW-T taxonomy was created by Mathias Bonduel (KU Leuven) during his PhD research (2017-2020) funded by the Research Foundation Flanders in the form of a Strategic Basic Research grant (grant agreement 1S65917N).
